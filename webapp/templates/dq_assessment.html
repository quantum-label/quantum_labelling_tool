Quality Assessment

Follow the instructions below to evaluate your dataset
  • Categories: Click on each EHDS category to expand and view its dimensions.
  • Dimensions: Inside each category, you will find the related dimensions. These are specific areas of data quality that need to be assessed. Click on each Dimension to access detailed information and view the metrics to evaluate.
  • Metrics: For each dimension, you will see one or two metrics. Some allow you to select predefined options, while others may require manual input.
  • Weights and Scores: Pay attention to the weighting of each dimension and metric, as they will affect the overall data quality score.
  • Assessment: This is a self-assessment tool. The value chosen from the dropdown menu for each metric should be based on your own calculations and practices. The system will then calculate the overall score based on your inputs.
  • !! Update: Click the "Save changes" button to save your progress. !!
  • RDF Certificate: At any point in time you can click on the "Download RDF" button to save locally your assessment in RDF (ttl) format. It will show the current state of the assessment.
  • Privacy: This system does not utilize or require access to your data, and it operates in full compliance with GDPR regulations.

Glossary

Within a Dimension:
  • Dimension Weight: (i.e. 10 out of 100) This represents the weight of the dimension in the overall quality score calculation.
    • For example, a weighting of 10% means that a score of 50 in this dimension contributes 5 points to the overall quality score.
Within a Metric:
  • Weight: The relative importance of each metric within a dimension, used to calculate the dimension's final score.
  • Measurement Approach & Suggested Formula: Represent suggested methods and formulas for measuring the metric (not mandatory).
